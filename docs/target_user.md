# Target User — research & validation

> Branched from [IDEA.md](IDEA.md). Validates Put's stated assumptions about who this product is for. Last updated 2026-05-01.

---

## TL;DR

Put's user description is mostly right, but the *moment in time* is wrong. The "rising Thai retail investor" narrative from 2020-2021 has flipped: SET hit a 7-year low in mid-2025, trading value is at 20-year lows, and a string of scandals (MORE, STARK, EA, JKN, Thonburi) burned a lot of the new cohort. The opportunity is not "ride a rising tide" — it's "**re-engage the 1.5-2M disillusioned investors who opened accounts during the boom and now need a reason to come back or stay**."

## Put's assumptions vs. evidence

### A1. Thai retail investing has grown a lot, driven by low-minimum apps

**Verdict:** Validated for 2020-2021, refined for 2026.

- New individual SET accounts: ~47K (2019) → ~119K (2020) → 230K in **just H1 2021**. By June 2021: 1.96M retail investors / 4.67M trading accounts. 98-99% of orders via internet/mobile. ([Thailand NOW](https://www.thailandnow.in.th/business-investment/retail-investors-the-rise-of-new-powerhouse-investors-in-the-thai-capital-market/))
- Mobile-first low-cost brokers are real and proliferated 2020-2022: Liberator (zero-commission), KKP Dime (fractional US from ฿50), StashAway, Webull Thailand, Jitta. ([Alpaca on Dime](https://alpaca.markets/blog/dime-revolutionizing-investing-in-thailand-with-alpaca/), [Liberator no-fee app](https://www.nationthailand.com/business/trading-investment/40024179))
- **But:** domestic retail's share of SET volume dropped from ~50% to 30-35% over the past decade. Real trading value is at 20-year lows in 2025. ([McKinsey on Thai capital market](https://www.mckinsey.com/featured-insights/future-of-asia/countries-and-regions/southeast-asia/southeast-asia-perspectives/improving-thailands-capital-market-competitiveness-and-efficiency), [Thai Stock Market Outlook](https://www.thailand-business-news.com/markets/207300-thai-stock-market-outlook))

### A2. New investors are mostly novice

**Verdict:** Validated.

- BoT/NSO 2020 financial literacy: 71/100 overall, **knowledge sub-score only 62.9/100** (the weakest dimension). ([Nation Thailand](https://www.nationthailand.com/business/40008144))
- Only **2.6% of Thais** invest their savings; 87.5% sit in bank accounts. ([Bangkok Post on literacy survey](https://www.bangkokpost.com/business/general/2205727/survey-shows-low-but-rising-financial-literacy))
- Krungsri 2025: 80% of respondents kept savings in bank accounts; 26% bought stocks/bonds/funds. ([Krungsri 2025 saving survey](https://www.krungsri.com/en/research/research-intelligence/thai-saving-2025))
- Thai investors are *under-confident* (not overconfident) per ASEAN comparative work — consistent with novices who know they don't know.

### A3. Vague goals ("grow my money", "make money work")

**Verdict:** Insufficient direct evidence — **research gap to fill via interviews.**

- Adjacent data: Krungsri 2025 top saving motivations are "emergencies," "travel," "treat oneself" — short-term and consumption-flavored. Retirement acknowledged by ~70% but only 33% translate into actual investing.
- SET's own framing of the 2020-2021 boom: people viewed investing "as a new or additional way to make money and grow their wealth" — vague growth, not goal-based.
- **No public survey asks Thai retail investors directly about specific financial goals.** This is a great prompt for primary user interviews.

### A4. Trade on vibe and news (TV, word-of-mouth)

**Verdict:** Strongly validated — both behaviorally and via incidents.

- **The MORE Corporation pump-and-dump (Nov 2022)** is the textbook case: coordinated manipulation that pulled retail in via momentum/news. Estimated investor losses **>4.5B baht**. Triggered margin and trading-rule overhauls. ([Bangkok Post](https://www.bangkokpost.com/business/general/3242179/more-return-share-manipulation-case-moves-forward), [Nation Thailand](https://www.nationthailand.com/business/banking-finance/40060389))
- Subsequent scandals: **STARK** (accounting fraud), **JKN Global** (collapse), **EA / Energy Absolute** (fraud charges, ~95% share-price collapse), **Thonburi Healthcare** (phantom projects). All caught retail money based on hype, not fundamentals. ([Crisis of Confidence](https://www.nationthailand.com/business/banking-finance/40060447))
- Information environment dominated by Pantip, Stock2Morrow Facebook, efinanceThai, LINE groups, finfluencers.

### A5. Don't actively monitor portfolios — check weekly or less, miss timing

**Verdict:** Mixed. **Adjacent literature suggests app users actually over-check.** Research gap on Thai-specific data.

- Global studies (Liu et al. on China; Indonesia mobile broker study) show mobile apps *increase* trading frequency and checking. Notifications + easy access induce more trading. ([INFORMS — Mobile Apps & Trading Behaviors](https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/isre.2020.0616))
- Likely a population split: an *active* engaged segment over-checks; a *dormant* segment of "opened account in COVID, went quiet" investors under-checks. The product's target may be the dormant segment.
- **Action:** put a literal question in the interview script — "How often do you open your brokerage app? When was the last time you bought or sold? What made you do it?"

### A6. Don't know portfolio-management practices

**Verdict:** Validated indirectly.

- Knowledge sub-score 62.9 covers basic concepts (interest, inflation, diversification) — not the harder stuff (asset allocation, rebalancing, position sizing).
- Even *advised* HNW clients show home-bias / under-diversification (only 20-25% international exposure). The unadvised retail investor is presumably worse. ([Asian Private Banker](https://asianprivatebanker.com/insights/asia-markets-2024/thailand-aum/))
- Academic study on SET retail trading: dominates volume, loses to institutional/foreign on timing and short-term returns. ([Investor type trading behavior on SET](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0927538X11000424))

## Things Put didn't ask about that change the picture

### B1. The retail boom is OVER. Reframe needed.

- SET hit ~1,054 in mid-2025 vs. 1,852 peak in 2018. Foreign net sells of ~108B baht in first 10 months of 2025. ([Chiang Rai Times](https://www.chiangraitimes.com/finance/thailands-struggling-set/))
- New SET CEO has explicitly framed the job as "rebuilding investor confidence." ([Kaohoon — New SET Chief](https://www.kaohooninternational.com/markets/545395))
- **The product narrative shifts:** from "ride the retail wave" to "**re-engage the burned cohort**" — 1.5-2M people with accounts who don't trust the market or themselves anymore.

### B2. Demographics: ~60% Gen Y, urban, mid-income

- The 2020-2022 cohort skewed Gen Y (then 25-41), Bangkok-heavy, tech-native. Average above-average-income Gen Y monthly income ~฿30K. ([EIC — Capturing Thai Gen Y](https://www.scbeic.com/th/detail/product/488))
- Gen Z is now entering but is more **crypto-curious** than equity-curious (42% of Bitcoin-interested Thais plan to invest within a year). ([Ipsos](https://www.ipsos.com/en-th/42-thais-who-are-interested-bitcoin-plan-invest-within-1-year))
- Implication: distinguish *Gen Y first-time SET buyers* from *Gen Z first-time Bitkub buyers* — they overlap but anchor differently.

### B3. Asset class strategic question

A scoping question — which asset classes does the product cover, and in what order?

| Asset | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| **SET stocks** | Largest user base, most painful gap | Multi-year drawdown, broken trust, hard narrative |
| **Fractional US stocks** (Dime's wedge) | Clean consumer story, US bull market, bypasses SET trust problem | Dime + Webull TH already there; moat must be insight, not access |
| **Crypto** | Massive engaged user base (Bitkub 300K+) | Different reg/risk frame; dilutes "investment" positioning |
| **Mutual funds** (RMF/SSF/Thai ESG) | Tax-incentivized, where novices actually park money | Less sexy; less crowded for AI advice |

### B4. "Scandal protection" as a sharp wedge

The MORE/STARK/EA fallout created a clear unmet need: **avoid getting wrecked by the next scandal stock**. AI is well-suited to this (anomaly detection on financials, ownership concentration, related-party transactions, sudden volume changes). May be sharper than generic "growth advice."

### B5. Regulatory tailwind

SEC Thailand and SET are actively trying to rebuild confidence and have tightened rules post-MORE. There may be openness to AI-driven *investor-protection / education* products — possibly even partnership / distribution opportunities through SET's own channels (SET Education, Settrade). ([OECD Capital Market Review of Thailand 2025](https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/oecd-capital-market-review-of-thailand-2025_0a975590-en.html))
